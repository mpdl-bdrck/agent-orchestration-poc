# Business Strategy

## Overview

This document outlines Bedrock Platform's comprehensive business strategy, including our vision, market approach, product portfolio, go-to-market strategy, and strategic priorities. It serves as the foundational guide for business development, product planning, and strategic decision-making across all organizational functions.

## Vision & Mission

### Vision Statement
```yaml
Vision:
"Bedrock Platform provides a highly customizable, commercially flexible, and media agnostic AdTech platform that enables programmatic activation of specialized data and media businesses."

Mission:
"To empower verticalized ad networks and data-rich businesses with the programmatic advertising technology they need to monetize unique assets and create differentiated business models."
```

### Core Beliefs
```yaml
Foundational Beliefs:
1. Transformation Inevitability:
   - All advertising media will eventually be bought programmatically via OpenRTB
   - Digital advertising is becoming addressable and programmatic
   - Recent trends: TV → CTV, DOOH → pDOOH transitions
   - OpenRTB as the universal protocol for ad trading

2. Pioneer Market Opportunity:
   - Innovation driven by pioneering users and businesses
   - New business models emerging in programmatic advertising
   - Current platforms underserve or are incompatible with unique models
   - Bedrock aims to serve and partner with these pioneers
```

## Market Strategy

### Target Market Definition

#### Primary Target Market: Verticalized Ad Networks
```yaml
Market Characteristics:
Unique Value Propositions:
  - Proprietary data assets for specific advertiser verticals
  - Specialized capabilities (retail, automotive, travel, etc.)
  - Business models incompatible with standard DSP offerings
  - Need for customization and operational flexibility

Market Examples:
  - Barter ad networks with unique inventory arrangements
  - Forward purchasing networks with specialized buying models
  - Data-rich publishers seeking programmatic monetization
  - Vertical-specific technology companies entering advertising

Market Size:
  - Serviceable Addressable Market: $35 billion
  - Target Market Share: 0.05% by 2026
  - Revenue Target: $300M media spend, $50M ARR
```

#### Secondary Target Market: Independent Agencies & SME Advertisers
```yaml
Market Characteristics:
Growth Opportunities:
  - Seeking differentiated capabilities and data access
  - Cost-conscious but performance-focused buyers
  - Need for white-label or co-branded solutions
  - Desire for transparent and flexible commercial models

Market Access Strategy:
  - Leverage data and capabilities built for primary market
  - Provide springboard for broader market expansion
  - Focus on unique value propositions unavailable elsewhere
  - Maintain cost-effective service delivery model
```

### Competitive Positioning

#### Market Gap Analysis
```yaml
Competitive Vacuum Created by Acquisitions:
Historical Leaders (Now Acquired):
  - Beeswax (acquired by Comcast 2021) - deprioritized
  - BidCore (acquired by Criteo 2021) - integrated into larger platform
  - Market opportunity gap for customizable DSP solutions

Current Market Gaps:
  - Lack of viable alternatives for specialized use cases
  - Limited options for vertical-specific programmatic solutions
  - Gap in flexible, customizable programmatic platforms
  - Opportunity for modern technology stack with startup agility
```

#### Differentiation Strategy
```yaml
Core Differentiators:
Technical Superiority:
  - Modern, cloud-native architecture vs. legacy systems
  - Real-time processing with sub-100ms latency
  - Comprehensive API-first design
  - Advanced customization and flexibility capabilities

Commercial Innovation:
  - Multiple pricing models (QPS, revenue share, custom)
  - Transparent fee structure and reporting
  - Lower barriers to entry than enterprise DSPs
  - Aligned incentives with client success

Market Focus:
  - Deep understanding of verticalized ad network needs
  - Specialized features for unique business models
  - White-label and co-branded platform capabilities
  - Agile development responsive to market needs
```

## Product Strategy

### Product Portfolio Architecture

#### Three-Product Strategy
```yaml
Product Portfolio:
1. Bedrock Supply (Direct):
   - Direct publisher integrations via Prebid adapter
   - Prioritized demand routing for private and investment deals
   - Publisher-focused revenue optimization
   - Direct inventory access and control

2. Bedrock Curation:
   - Data provider monetization platform
   - Bid stream curation through shaping and enhancement
   - Real-time data activation and enrichment
   - Custom audience and targeting capabilities

3. Bedrock Bidder:
   - Bespoke Demand Side Bidder instances
   - Custom bidding logic and machine learning
   - Campaign management and optimization
   - White-label user interface and APIs
```

### Product Development Philosophy

#### Product Tenets
```yaml
Core Product Principles:
1. Launch Fast, Learn Faster:
   - Build small, usable versions first
   - Iterate based on real usage and business impact
   - Velocity with tight feedback loops over speculative perfection

2. Build What Moves the Metrics:
   - Every product investment tied to measurable outcomes
   - Focus on performance, efficiency, margin, adoption
   - Business value over "nice to have" features

3. Data Must Drive Decisions:
   - Surface timely, granular, trustworthy data
   - Reporting, forecasting, optimization as core products
   - Data-driven decision making at all levels

4. Revenue Impact Is Engineering Responsibility:
   - Monetization levers as engineering priorities
   - Business logic embedded in product core
   - Pacing, bidding, budget control as primary focus
```

#### Technical Strategy
```yaml
Engineering Approach:
Performance & Scale:
  - Sub-second latency and global reliability requirements
  - Design for QPS spikes and network failures
  - Infrastructure scaling without speed compromise

Modularity & Future-Proofing:
  - Flexible, composable systems adaptable to change
  - Support for new IDs, auction formats, regulations
  - Avoid full rewrites for market evolution

User Experience Focus:
  - Simple interfaces over complex systems
  - Clean, consistent APIs and internal tools
  - Fast, intuitive reporting and workflows
```

## Commercial Strategy

### Business Model Innovation

#### Flexible Pricing Models
```yaml
Pricing Strategy Innovation:
QPS-Based Pricing:
  - Pay for queries per second rather than media spend
  - Predictable costs aligned with technical usage
  - Suitable for high-efficiency, low-waste campaigns
  - Competitive advantage for sophisticated buyers

Revenue Share Models:
  - Percentage of incremental revenue generated
  - Aligned incentives with client success
  - Lower barriers to entry for new clients
  - Scalable pricing as business grows

Custom Commercial Arrangements:
  - Tailored pricing for unique business models
  - Flexible minimum spend requirements
  - White-label licensing options
  - Partnership and revenue-sharing opportunities
```

#### Revenue Optimization
```yaml
Revenue Strategy:
Multiple Revenue Streams:
1. Monthly Recurring License Fees:
   - Base platform access and usage fees
   - Tiered pricing based on usage and features
   - Minimum commitments with volume discounts

2. CPM Uplift Charges:
   - Percentage of media spend for prepay customers
   - Transparent fee structure and reporting
   - Competitive rates vs. enterprise DSPs

3. Data Usage Fees:
   - SOW and CPM-based data activation fees
   - Third-party data provider revenue sharing
   - Custom data integration and processing fees

4. Professional Services:
   - Custom implementation and integration services
   - Training and support services
   - Consulting and optimization services
```

## Risk Management & Mitigation

### Strategic Risks

#### Competitive Risks
```yaml
Risk Assessment & Mitigation:
Large DSP Response:
Risk: Major DSPs develop competitive customization capabilities
Mitigation: 
  - Maintain technology and innovation leadership
  - Deepen client relationships and switching costs
  - Focus on underserved market segments
  - Continuous product differentiation

New Entrant Competition:
Risk: Well-funded startups targeting same market
Mitigation:
  - First-mover advantage and market positioning
  - Strong client relationships and case studies
  - Continuous innovation and feature development
  - Strategic partnerships and exclusive relationships
```

#### Market Risks
```yaml
Market Evolution Risks:
Technology Disruption:
Risk: New technologies making current approach obsolete
Mitigation:
  - Modular architecture enabling rapid adaptation
  - Continuous technology research and development
  - Strategic partnerships with emerging technology providers
  - Investment in next-generation capabilities

Regulatory Changes:
Risk: Privacy and advertising regulations impacting business model
Mitigation:
  - Proactive compliance and privacy-by-design approach
  - Legal and regulatory expertise development
  - Industry participation and standard setting
  - Flexible architecture supporting regulatory requirements
```

### Operational Risks

#### Execution Risks
```yaml
Operational Risk Management:
Technical Execution:
Risk: Platform performance or reliability issues
Mitigation:

  - Continuous monitoring and alerting systems

Market Execution:
```

## Conclusion

Bedrock Platform's business strategy positions the company to capture significant market opportunity in the programmatic advertising space by serving underserved verticalized ad networks and data-rich businesses. Through a combination of technical excellence, commercial innovation, and market focus, Bedrock can establish a strong competitive position and achieve sustainable growth.

The key to success lies in executing on immediate market opportunities while building long-term competitive advantages through technology leadership, deep client relationships, and strategic partnerships. Continuous monitoring of market evolution and competitive dynamics will ensure the strategy remains relevant and effective over time.
